About

I am a PhD candidate in the Artificial Intelligence Interdisciplinary Program at Seoul National University (SNU), advised by Prof. Youngki Lee in the Human-Centered Computer Systems Lab.

I am interested in how large language models and mixed reality interfaces can be applied to build usable, personalized, and socially grounded systems. My work is driven by a commitment to designing technologies that are transparent, supportive, and aligned with human needs in everyday settings.

My research focuses on developing situated support systems that assist users in real-world tasks—particularly in early education and collaborative virtual environments. I build systems that respond to user input, task progress, and interaction context to provide graduated assistance that balances user support with context-sensitive privacy control.

Research Themes

  • Situated support systems for education and collaboration
  • Scaffolding and graduated assistance for child–caregiver storytelling
  • Privacy-aware interaction in virtual and mixed reality spaces
  • Human-AI collaboration using large language models

Questions I Care About

  • How can systems detect where users are in a task and what kind of help they need?
  • What does it mean to protect privacy in collaborative virtual environments?
  • How can we scaffold children’s language development in meaningful ways?
  • How do we make LLMs more reliable, transparent, and usable in educational and social contexts?
